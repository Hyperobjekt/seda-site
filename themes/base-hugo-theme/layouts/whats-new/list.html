What's new in version 4.0

Explore more, updated data at your fingertips.

New features in the data

Version 4.0 of SEDA is updated with better estimates and cleaner data. New features allow you to access:

  • New crosswalk with stable school, district, and county IDs.
  • Data for 2016-2017 and 2017-2018 school years.
  • Data for more Native American students.
  • Estimates for Bureau of Indian Education (BIE) schools.
  • Math scores for Puerto Rico.
  • Insights and learning: Click here to see our latest discoveries.
  • Margins of error for each location's data
  • State-level views

New features in the Educational Opportunity Explorer

Refine your view of the data with new filtering options

Use filter controls within the Explorer to see places within your chosen ranges for a given metric, such as Average Test Scores or Socioeconomic Status. You can also filter by type of district (rural, town, suburban, or urban) or school (charter, magnet, and more).

  • Filter estimates by:
    • School, district, and county size
    • Average test score range
    • Learning rate range
    • Trend in test score range
    • Socioeconomic status range
    • Percent (%) qualifying for free or reduced priced lunch (FRPL) range
  • Select all schools in a particular district:
    • For example: Click here to see estimates for all schools in New York City Public Schools.
  • Select schools by characteristic:
    • Area: rural, town, suburban, and urban schools.
    • School type: public, charter, and magnet schools.
    • School age group: middle, elementary, and combined.

View and compare locations in new ways

We’ve refined the location data panel to provide a clearer view of statistics at a glance, and clicking any variable in the panel now updates the map and charts. You can also compare several locations’ data together in a revised table display.
